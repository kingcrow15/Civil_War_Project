The civil war occupies a unique place in U.S. history, it had the highest Death toll of any war in U.S. history. And the ripples of that cultural rift can still be felt today. Recently the discussion over some of the monuments of that war has come to the forefront of political debate in this country. In an effort to spotlight this trend, we are looking at the proliferation of these monuments since the civil war. And the effort to remove confederate monuments in recent years, as a measure of shifting cultural values in this country.
